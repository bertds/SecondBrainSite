Author: Mike Gastin

Summary

Three main takeaways :

  • writing is thinking
  • the value of an external system
  • the superiority of an emergent approach

Notes

the actual process of the writing is the thinking. don't just copy text you read but think about it by writing about it.
Need to store our thinking information outside of our brain because we forget. We store our thinking on 'cards' to be able to link them together, make network, ...

  • Fleeting notes : daily notes. Weekly review them to see if you want to make final notes.
  • Literature note while reading something (pagenumber - own ideas of what you read)
  • Final notes : definitive notes - which come in your zettelkasten. Refine them. Build your db of ideas.
    With notes you cover a lot of topics. Every day you could get another interest and work on that topic in your db. Layer by layer you add inforlmation to your zettelkasten to eventually produce articles, books.

Benefit ? Getting more out of books. Takes a bit longer. More insights, more original own ideas.
